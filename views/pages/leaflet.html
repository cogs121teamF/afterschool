Why afterschool activities matter


The importance of what children do after school is becoming more emphasized as it becomes a more well-known fact that children cannot develop from school alone, and the time children spend out of school is a significant amount. Children can potentially learn so much after school that they cannot learn from sitting through their school classes, yet it can be difficult to find good places for children to go to.

The Problem
trending_up

1 in 3 American children are overweight or obese.

What was not a problem decades ago is a problem today because children live an inactive lifestyle. Children rarely need to walk. Most time after school are spent on the screen.

1 in 4 American children are on their own after school.

That is 1,133,989 children on their own in the afternoon hours who are in need of finding beneficial activities to do.

schedule

Students spend 9 hours on media every day.

Studies have shown that excessive media use can lead to attention problems, school difficulties, sleep and eating disorders, and obesity.

Benefits of (Better) Afterschool Activities
grade

Aids Development

Children who participate in after school activities that engage their intellect or physical bodies benefit much more socially, physically, and psychologically than the children who spend their time on media.

trending_down

Decreases Stress

Children feel stressed as adults do, but the issue is that they do not know how to deal with it. After school activities can play a significant role in relieving stress through a fun and low pressure environment.

supervisor_account

Develops Skills

Children can grow in their self-esteem by learning new skills and interacting with their peers. For example, reading as a child has a positive correlation with the folowing: communication skills, logical thinking skills, concentration, and discipline.

Sources: Afterschool Alliance, Pew Research Center, She Knows, Early Moments

About Us

Afterschool helps parents find fun, educational and safe activities for their children to engage in. With afterschool, parents can help their children use their most crucial developmental years to get involved with their community, learn new skills and mature.

  • mode_edit Libraries have a multitude of books children can read and often provide activities for children to participate in.
  • location_on Local parks are great places to get your children outdoors with no additional activity costs.
  • supervisor_account Recreational centers have a variety of activities that range from crafts to sports that children can become a part of. They can learn new skills and make friends.
Afterschool aims to:
  • provide places primary students can go to after school
  • support all families with children in San Diego
  • keep primary students safe and productive
  • benefit the community by promoting well-being and family unity
